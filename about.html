Proclaiming the risen Christ, with Faith & Unity

Run for Jesus is an evangelical and ecumenical roadshow dedicated to sharing the message of Christ's Crucifixion and Resurrection. Through vibrant community events, we unite Christian youth from diverse backgrounds, fostering fellowship and faith.

ABOUT RUN FOR JESUS

The Run for Jesus Rally is a Christian evangelical and ecumenical event held on Holy Saturday, the day between Good Friday and Easter, to publicly proclaim the message of Jesus Christ's death and resurrection, with the necessary permission from the local government.

This rally brings together members of various churches within a locality, fostering unity and spiritual fervour. Participants, including youth, families, church leaders, and bikers, take to the streets running, walking, or riding along designated routes proclaiming the Easter message:

"Jesus has risen! Indeed, He has risen!"

The event features live music, vibrant banners, and enthusiastic participation, drawing public, media, and government attention to the significance of Easter and the Christian faith.

Ultimately, the Run for Jesus Rally serves as a public witness of faith, a demonstration of Christian unity, and an outreach initiative, ensuring that the message of Easter resonates across streets, towns, and cities.

HISTORY TIMELINE

Historical Development of Run for Jesus (RFJ)

The Run for Jesus rally, a movement dedicated to proclaiming the message of Jesus Christ's death and resurrection, has grown significantly over the years while staying true to its core mission.

2011

A Local Evangelical Rally

Run for Jesus was envisioned in 2011, launching in 30 locations with enthusiastic participation from young members of various churches, quickly becoming a vibrant and dynamic event.

2012 - 2017

A Regional Ecumenical Movement

Building on the success of Run for Jesus in 2011, the Aradana TV team expanded the rally to over 100 locations across the Telugu States, shaping RFJ into a truly ecumenical movement.

2017 - 2024

A National Christian Rally

With growing momentum, Run for Jesus expanded beyond the Telugu States into multiple states. The event now aims to be held in 600+ locations, bringing 650K+ participants together.

2025

A Global Christian Movement

Run for Jesus has reached international audiences through rallies in the United Kingdom, United States, Australia, New Zealand, and other countries, establishing RFJ as a worldwide movement.
